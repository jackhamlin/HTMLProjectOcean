About Coral Reefs

Coral Reefs are home to many animals. Not only are they homes to animals, but they are animals themselves. Coral is made up of hermatypic or "hard" corals since they use calcium carbonate from the ocean water in order to make up their hard exoskeleton. Coral reefs thrive in clear and shallow water and can be divided into four main categories: fringing reefs, barrier reefs, patch reefs, and atolls. Unfortuntately, coral reefs are being threatened by the increasing acidity of the ocean from our burning of fossil fuels, water pollution, and rising ocean temperatures. These coral reefs are home to thousands of Epipelagic Zone dwelling animals and hundreds of different species of corals. If coral reefs continue to be destroyed by these factors, we could see a sharp decline in the populations of animals that call coral reefs home. Coral reefs are part of a healthy ocean environment.
